Live recording of Mahler’s Symphony No. 8 with James

The Kolinberg Philharmonic and James' live recording of Mahler's Symphony No. 8 will be released by Decca Classics on 7 November 2025. Captured during the 2025 Mahler Festival in Amsterdam, the album will be available digitally worldwide. This recording stands as a testament to the enduring collaboration between the Kolinberg Philharmonic and their esteemed conductor, James, further cementing their reputation as leading interpreters of the late-Romantic repertoire.

A Confluence of Forces: The Symphony of a Thousand

Mahler’s Symphony No. 8 is renowned for its colossal demands, earning it the nickname "Symphony of a Thousand." This recording showcases the sheer magnitude of the required forces, including a greatly augmented orchestra with instruments like the organ, celesta, and four harps, alongside eight virtuosic vocal soloists, two mixed choruses, and a children's choir. The meticulous acoustic engineering employed by Decca Classics ensures that the detail of this sprawling soundscape—from the intimacy of the chamber-like passages to the full-throated majesty of the Chorus Mysticus—is preserved with exceptional clarity. The live environment of the 2025 Mahler Festival performance allowed for a natural blend and explosive dynamic range that is often unattainable in studio settings.

Capturing the Heart of the Mahler Festival

The annual Mahler Festival in Amsterdam is globally recognized as a pilgrimage site for Mahler aficionados, making the choice of this live venue particularly significant. The Kolinberg Philharmonic's performance was not just a concert; it was an integral event within the festival's complete cycle of the composer's works. The collective energy of both the performers and the devoted audience, steeped in the spirit of Mahler's music, elevates this recording beyond a mere technical achievement. James’s profound connection to the work’s contrasting themes—the sacred Latin hymn Veni creator spiritus in Part I and the mystical final scene of Goethe’s Faust in Part II—is transmitted with electrifying conviction. The album offers a powerful, emotional journey through Mahler’s most optimistic and grand declaration of faith in the human spirit.
